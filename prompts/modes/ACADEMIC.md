## Synthetic Example

**Research Context:** "Analyze academic communication about AI safety"

**Example Query:**
> "What does the research say about AI alignment challenges?"

**Example Response for Analysis:**
> AI alignment has been extensively studied in the research literature. Hubinger et al. (2019) introduced the concept of "deceptive alignment," where systems may appear aligned while pursuing different objectives internally. This builds on earlier work by Yudkowsky on instrumental convergence—the tendency for diverse agents to converge on similar sub-goals.
> 
> The field has identified several key challenges:
> 
> 1. **Outer alignment:** Specifying the right objective
> 2. **Inner alignment:** Ensuring the model actually pursues that objective
> 3. **Reward hacking:** Gaming the specified metric without achieving intended goals
> 
> Recent work by Anthropic and others has explored constitutional AI and other approaches to address these challenges. However, as Bostrom (2014) notes in "Superintelligence," these remain open research problems with significant implications.
> 
> It's worth noting that this research is ongoing, and many proposed solutions remain theoretical rather than empirically validated.
